I guess I’m getting older… (food on holidays)

in Uncategorized

on 25 February 2015

More and more people ask me what I eat here, in Peru and Bolivia, complaining that I don’t post enough food photos. Peruvian cuisine is considered to be one of the best on the continent, and to be honest I kind of regret I hadn’t stayed there longer. Here in Bolivia I’m not so lucky. I used to be a big fan of street food but it’s been changing recently. I really don’t know how to explain that: am I getting older and I need to sit at a table, have I tried too much of street food in too many places or maybe as my cooking skills got better over the years I want to eat better food? That’s what I was thinking about yesterday sitting in a smart restaurant serving… ekhm… Brasilian dishes (recommended by Lonely Planet, a really good recommendation).


I might seem to be very fussy to you right now, so let me tell you that one day before I ate at a local market. My almuerzo (lunch) doesn’t look bad in the photos, but believe me, it was edible… nothing more. The only advantage was price was price, about 2 dollars with a pepsi.


And finally the dessert I bought sitting on the stairs in front of La Paz cathedral. I know many of you would never want to try it, but as a big fan of warm ice-cream I really enjoyed it (I forgot the name, but it’s similar to some kind of meringue cream).

PS. It’s not true I eat only potatoes, I like quinoa as well.
