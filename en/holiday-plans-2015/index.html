Holiday plans 2015.

in Uncategorized

on 18 January 2015

Once I made the decision of not going to Latin America one more time I felt lost. It was Latin American that made me look forward to my holiday break so much. I was hesitant for a while wondering if it was a good idea to give up this destination, but five times almost in a row is enough, isn’t it?

Peru
Boliwia

In 2014 I visited to of my dream destinations in South America: Machu Picchu i Peru and Salar de Uyuni in Bolivia – big dreams came true – time to change the destination.

Where should I go? The decision was easy and difficult at the same time.

Easy because what else could I choose but Africa?
Difficult, because you need to choose the right country. I’ve always dreamt of visiting three M countries: Mali, Malawi and Madagascar, but… There’s an armed conflict and ebola virus in the first country, the second one is a kind of a small country that ‘nobody’ knows about, so it’s hard to convince somebody to join you in your trip. Madagascar? Well, it sounded just right…

Who should I go with?

While I didn’t want any travel companions in Latin America as I much preferred to wander on my own, make my own decisions ans speak Spanish, I didn’t want to go alone to Africa. There were two reasons for that:
– there aren’t many tourists or backpackers there so you can’t find a cheap bed in a common hostel room – you need to pay more for decent accommodation if you’re are alone, and you need to pay more for trips, because it might be difficult to find other people to join a group,
– although I’ve already been twice in Africa (Ghana, Togo, Benin and Ethiopia and Somaliland) and I knew I would manage on my own without problems, I still remembered that many times I was fed up because hard as I tried, cultural differences were sometimes difficult to overcome – I decided I wanted to share these inconveniences with someone.

It’s not easy to find the right travel companions.

The reasons vary, but the most common ones are following:
– an average person has 26 days of paid holidays a year (and my plan was to go away for a month);
– money – we might try to go budget, but still, going outside Europe is expensive (a ticket costs 600 USD minimum);
– many people are terrified of going to Africa, they imagine it’s the continent of poverty, diseases, starvation and hostility;
Those who have time, money and are not afraid of going to Africa usually would rather go to Asia…

However, if you keep repeating that Africa is what you want, people might get used to hearing the name and they will accept it and this is how I convinced three people to go to Madagascar with me.
But then I made a big mistake: fearing they might have second thoughts learning the airfare to Magadascar I came up with optional destinations, so, all in all, the choice was:
1. Picturesque safari in Tanzania and white beaches of Zanzibar,
2. African backpacking in Malawi
3. Baobabs and lemurs in Madagascar and white beaches of Madagascar
4. Mighty waterfalls of Zambia and Zimbabwe.

The vast majority of my Facebook friends chose Magadascar as their hypothetical dream destination, but not my prospective travel companions – once they heard the word ‘safari’, anything else I said fell on deaf ears.
I might’ve argued more, but the plane tickets to Tanzania were the cheapest option.

It’s vital to have reliable travel companions. It’s better to go on your own than with people you don’t trust or won’t get on well with. We’ve already been on a trip to Georgia once so I know we’re going to have a great time!

PLAN: Tanzania
Time: 1 month (July/August)
Highlights: safari (Serengeti and NgoroNgroro) and white beaches of Zanzibar
Money: airfare: around 600usd, safari price: minimum of 150-200usd per day (time to start saving to have 3-4 days there), the rest is going to be (will have to be) budget: local food, local buses, cheap accommodation.

The decision is made, the tickets are bought, now I just look forward to my holidays.
I also know I’ll need to answer numerous questions, just like every time I go somewhere.

Frequently Asked Questions:
Can’t you go somewhere in Europe?
I could, but I don’t want.
Don’t you think you should get to know your country better before you travel somewhere far away?
I try to travel around Poland whenever I can, and I regret I can’t do it more, but I love spending my holidays in a place where a different language is spoken, where I can experience different culture, and which is so different than what I have on everyday basis.
Why to Africa?
Because it’s less touristy and I know little about the countries there. Besides, it feels as if someone lifted my from my sofa and put straight into a National Geographic travel program – this is how I felt even in big cities and I like the feeling, I simply like the idea of something less familiar.
Where is Tanzania?
In western Africa.
Will you communicate in English?
I hope so.
Are you getting vaccinated?
I have all the necessary vaccinations, yellow fever including.
Why do you get vaccinated? My friend went to ….  and nothing bad happened.
Well, nothing ever happened to me while travelling, but the funny thing is those vaccinations came in handy when I ended up in a Polish hospital.
Safari is going to be touristy, why are you going there if you prefer less touristy places.
I do prefer less touristy places, but it doesn’t mean I skip those popular ones. I don’t care how many people are going to be there – I want to see it anyway – so many people say it’s been an amazing experience – they can’t all be wrong.
Why don’t you climb Kilimanjaro?
I won’t because: a) it’s too expensive (much over 1000usd), b) it takes about a week, c) after my altitude sickness experience in Peru I’d rather stick to lower areas
Don’t you get bored lying on a beach? I would never do it.
Well, I love the picturesque view of a white beach and turquoise water and I can stare at it forever.
Besides I hardly every lie on the beach and in fact I’m going to dive in Zanzibar not really lie on the beach.
What else are you going to see/ do in Tanzania apart from safari and Zanzibar?
I don’t know yet, I’ll think of something. Probably just experience Africa off the beaten track that is move around, laze somewhere, look at people, have walks, take photos, relax.
Aren’t you afraid?
No, I’m not.
