Cuban cars.

in Uncategorized

on 19 March 2015

Fidel Castro gained power in 1959  and decided that import of cars would be impossible without the goverment permission. The following year president Dwight D.Eisenhower broke diplomatic relations with Cuba and imposed embargo on export.
In 1962 the United States introduced the total embargo, which wasn’t easy for Cuba, a country whose econome relied heavily on the US. Till 2014 trade relations between the two countries were non-existent.

On 17th December 2014 presidents Barack Obama and Raul Castro shook hands and declared the restoration of all relations between the countries.

There are around 60,000 old American cars called yank tanks or máquinas in the 11-million country. Cubans earn little and only some can afford cars so most of those American yank tanks are used as taxis. When the embargo was introduced in the 60s, import of car parts was impossible so everything depended on the ingenuity of Cuban mechanics.
Some cars are in a really good shape, others seem to be glued and on the verge of falling apart – just don’t be surprised if you’re forced to push your taxi!
