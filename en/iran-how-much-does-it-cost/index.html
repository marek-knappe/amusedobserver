Iran – how much does it cost?

in Uncategorized

on 21 April 2016


How long? 9 days
When? End of January 2016
How much money spent in Iran? about 500 Euro
Flight: 95 Euro (Berlin via Kiev to Teheran)
Type: independent travel, backpacking
Aim: sightseeing and food

“Was it expensive?” “How much did you pay for your trip?” These are the question that every traveller hears a lot. Some people would give you an average per day, others just give examples of prices, others still will only reveal their costs in private conversations.
Some time ago I decided to note down every cent I spent during my travels to check myself how much money I spent on what – I hope my cost list might help you a bit when organising your own trip.

 

Is Iran expensive?
Some of you might look at my costs and decide it wasn’t expensive, others will immediately say this kind of trip can be done much cheaper – all of you will probably be right!

First of all, accommodation – it all depends on what you expect – it can be much more expensive but also a lot cheaper. Couchsurfing is said to work really well in Iran, but I didn’t want to use it – when I have little time, no fixed itinerary and I want to see a lot CS is not the best idea for me.
We didn’t want to sleep in dorms as well, room with a bathroom was our only choice. First of all, it’s the basic comfort for me, secondly, it might be quite chilly in the mornings/evenings so I wouldn’t feel like leaving my room to take a shower, and finally, when you’re a woman in Iran, once you leave your room you need to be fully dressed and cover your head with a scarf so a bathroom outside a hotel room was not an option for us.

Sightseeing is not always worth the price. While you accept that you pay 200,000 Rials to enter Persepolis, the same price might seem really steep when paid at Necropolis or Pasargade, which, although also interesting, offer much less to a tourist.
The only place where I think that I wasted my money was the old post-office museum in Meybod.

I never economize on food – I’m a food aficionado and I do my best to try as much as I can – learning about the country through its cuisine is always one of the most fascinating thing to do in the places I visit. If you want to know what I eat in Iran, look at my article on PERSIAN CUISINE.

As far as the transport is concerned, well you need to have either time or money – twice we hired a taxi for the whole day and it was money really well spent as we managed to pack more into our itinerary.
