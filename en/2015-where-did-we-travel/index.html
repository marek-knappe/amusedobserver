2015 – where did we travel?

in Uncategorized

on 2 January 2016

“Where did you go abroad in 2015?”

– I asked both my friends and fellow travel bloggers and got answers from 179 people who made the total of 1174 trips!

On average a travel blogger made around seven trips, while my non-blogging friends travelled around 5 times. Not bad, right?
The oficial figures for Poland say that last year 32 per cent of society decided to travel abroad, while 68% spent their holidays at home. In the charts below I treated Poland as ‘abroad’ as some of my friends live outside Poland and for them holidays here are holidays aborad.

Travels on the map

First, let’s have a quick look at the maps.
My friends visited 30% of all 197 countries in the world.

As you can clearly see, travel bloggers were much more eager to answer the question about travels which may be due to two things: first, they love to boast a little bit, and also, they are called travel bloggers because travelling is their life!

My friends might travel less for different reasons. Some answered they would be happy to help, but they didn’t go abroad last year.

The most popular destinations

It turned out that the most popular destinations for both my friends and fellow travel bloggers are Germany and Italy – two countries that I’ve visited a couple of times but I keep saying there are not my favourite destinations – maybe I should try to get to know them better?
It’s really interesting that only one country from outside Europe made it to both top 10s – my friends often travel to the USA, while travel bloggers prefer Thailand.

The official statistic say that the most popular destinations for Polish people in 2015 were: Greece, Croatia, Italy, Spain and Turkey – no mention of Germany there which is really popular both with my friends and travel bloggers.
Germany might be a popular destination for many of my friends who live in Poznań – it’s so close to Germany that it makes a great day trip. How can I explain popularity of this country among travel bloggers? I have no idea. I was once scolded for saying it’s a boring country – I won’t say it again, I’m planning to go and find out what’s so good there!

In the top 10s I find two facts particularly interesting:
1. Both groups visit the Czech Republic pretty often, but only travel bloggers travel to the neighbouring Slovakia.
2. The popularity of Ukraine among travel bloggers – in all the discussions my friends mentioned the war and voiced concerns for safety,but travel bloggers decided to visit safe regions of this country benefiting from low prices when tourists deserted the region in fear for their safety.

But… travelling is expensive, isn’t it?

I’ve heard questions about money so many times. It comes up almost every time travelling is discussed, especially if we talk about faraway travels. Many travel blogges have tried to explain what they do to have more money for travelling. I’ve never written about it because for me it’s pretty simple: I work more and I save more.
I know that out of 179 people that participated in the questionnaire there are some who have travel-related jobs, others are quite well off but don’t have much time to travel, and there are also those who have time but don’t have much money so they travel slow on a shoestring.

Can you say you know the country after a weekend or a week?

Someone asked this question and I say ‘no, you can’t’, but this is not what this game is about: it’s not about how well we know the places we visited but how many we did visit. Every trip counted – be it a one day trip to a neighbouring country or a week or a month somewhere far away.

Africa – terra incognita

Have you noticed it as well? Africa is definitely a land less travelled!
Whenever I mention an African country I’ve visited or I’d love to visit I get strange looks and questions if I’m not afraid, if there’s anything at all to see there and so on.
Almost all the travel bloggers when planning their round-the-world trips, skip this continent. Why?
I want to express my gratitude to all my non-blogging friends and fellow travel bloggers for participating in my questionnaire. The group examined is by no means a representative group of the society, neither were any rules for preparing good questionnaires followed here. It was just for fun 🙂
