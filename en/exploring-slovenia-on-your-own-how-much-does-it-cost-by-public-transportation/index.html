Exploring Slovenia on your own – how much does it cost by public transportation?

in Uncategorized

on 17 June 2015

– You’re saying for six nights? Really? – the receptionist didn’t try to hide surprise in his voice.
Well, true. Why?

Just before my visit to Slovenia, I heard it was a small, expensive country and it’s best to visit it on your way to Croatia, just like that.
The reality? I was amazed what a lovely country Slovenia is with its pleasant cities, helpful people and beautiful views.

The entertainment and activities are always expensive, if you enjoy tasting wine, you pay, and touristy places are always more expensive – it’s true everywhere you go.

Today, you get a short overview of my expenses, more reports and photos of wonderful Slovenia will follow soon.

Flight: Poznan – Munich – Lublana – around €170
Accommodation: Penzion pod Lipo, twin room with a bathroom €51 (with a small discount as we stayed for six nights)
Transportation to/from the airport: €20/person

DAY 1 – LJUBLJANA €10 – castle (funicular and entrance) €5 – Ljubljana free tour  (it’s a tour for tips kind – you pay what you feel like at the end of the trip I you decide it is worth it) €3 – half a kilo of sweet cherries at the market
€8 – pizza (a big pizza – enough for two to share) + €2,60 0,5 litre beer (beere was just a little bit more expensive than coke or water – but it was bigger – good to drink on a hot summer day)
€8,5 – dinner (idrija zlikrofi – stuffed dumplings) + a glass of Malvazija wine
€3 – zakupy (watermelon, cheese, water)
TOTAL: €34,6

DAY 2 – PIRAN
€13,10 – bus to Piran
€11.10 – bus to Ljubljana
€1 – entrance to the bell tower
€1 – entrance to the city walls
€10 – lunch: grilled calmari + salad
€2,5 – beer
€3,9 – ice coffee
€1,22 – a small bottle of water + a small bottle of a soft drink (in a shop)
€8.80 – dinner: Slovenian sausage + wine (yes, I know it’s a strange mixture, but the wine was really good there)
TOTAL: €52

DAY 3 – CAVES: Postojna Cave + Skocjan  caves + Predjama Castle
€5,55 – train: Ljublajna – Postojna
€3,44 – train: Postojna – Divaca
€3,85 – train: Divaca – Lublana
€15/person (€30 total) taxi from Postojna Cave to Predjama Castle and back – we had no choice as we had very little time to fit everything into our schedule (were were even told before we wouldn’t manage to do everything we wanted using public transport – we did – but if you plan something like that a rented car is more recommended)
€29,90 – ticket to Postojna (castle included)
€16 – ticket to Skocjan Caves
€5 – breakfast bought in a shop (a coke, a croissant, a sandwitch)
TOTAL: €78,74

DAY 4 – Bled
€7 bus: Ljubljana – Bled
€6,30 bus: Bled – Ljubljana
€9 – castle
€4 – entrance to Vintgar gorge
€7,40 – lunch
€3.60 – famous Bled cream cake
€6 – dinner in a wine shop
TOTAL: €43,3

DAY 5 – Maribor + Ptuj
€19,29 – train ticket: Ljubljana – Maribor, return Ptuj – Ljubljana (weekend discount)
€3,50 – bus ticket from Maribor to Ptuj
€5 – castle in Ptuj
€3,25 – breakfast  (a coke + sweet buns)
€7,5 – lunch (plescovica)
€2,50 – beer
€2,80 – ice coffee
€7 – dinner in a wine bar in Ljubljana
TOTAL: €50,84

TOTAL COSTS in Slovenia:
Accommodation: €153
Transportation: €85 + to/from the airport: €20
Sightseeing: €80,90
Food and drink: €71.47 (drinks such as beer, wine, water, coke €28)
TOTAL: +/-€450 for five days/six nights (na miejscu)

Is it possible to make it cheaper?
Of course it is. You can save on:
a) accommodation: hostels are less expensive, double rooms (not twin rooms) are less expensive and
rooms without bathrooms will cost you less,
b) transportation – renting a car would definitely safe you time (and money if shared)
c) food and drink – you can definitely economize here, but it basically depends on what you like. Personally, I like tasting, trying, comparing, so I only save on food at home (and Slovenian wine proved to be good, so it was hard not to drink it)
