Culinary delights of Portugal

in Uncategorized

on 11 May 2016

 

I have only three culinary memories of my first visit to Portugal:
– onion soup eaten in a cosy restaurant in Porto on a chilly December evening,
– pasteis de nata from Belem (custard tarts) that I remembered as delicacy although I could no longer recall their taste,
– a bowl full of mules that I refused too eat although I was hungry (seafood? Disgusting, no way I would have eaten that!).

Years have passed and I went to Portugal again. I dreamt of pasteis de nata from Belem (Belem only, no other place would do) and seafood (my culinary tastes have changed a lot over the years).

Everybody keeps asking what I ate during my short visit, so a short report for you here, enjoy!

Portugal is famous for fish and seafood

– the consumption of fish per capita is the highest in Europe and one of the highest in the world so if you are fish lover this is the country to go!

Seafood

I started my culinary experience with two dishes that I would have never even tried a few years before: Amêijoas à Bulhão Pato or clams in wine sauce and shrimps. I used to dislike both, but I guess some things are an acquired taste.

After we quickly ate the dishes, we asked for more bread to clean very delicious sauces off the plates.

I wouldn’t be myself without trying my favourite seafood on the very first evening – something that I came to love from the first bite and is called in Portuguese lulas. Squid! 

Time for octopus.
Octopus is something I usually try to avoid – it is usually chewy and hard to bite. It was only last year in Tanzania that I tried octopus that was absolutely delicious – it was so good that I tasted it many times without getting bored and I called the best dish of the holiday.
Unfortunately, when I looked for those flavours in Europe I was unable to find them. The first attempts at Portuguese octopus was ok, but just ok, nothing more. It was only in Solar 31 da Calçada restaurant that I ate perfect octopus (on the right picture).
So, if you want to try good octopus go to this Lisbon place – you won’t regret it! (You might go to Tanzania as well, but Portugal is closer, so you stand a bigger chance of getting to Lisbon).

Fish

The chef of the above mentioned restaurant made a short presentation on what was available on the menu, explaining the difference in flavours and giving bits and pieces of intersting information. It was not only a great entertainment, but it also allowed us to make a good decision when ordering from the menu.

Black Scabbard fish (portug. Peixe Espada) is very characteristic for Portugal – do not confuse it with swordfish – and lives at the depth between 180 and 1800 meters. Because of that it’s a fish that you won’t see in its natural envirnonment. After we heard its meat is delicate and tasty we knew we would have to try it.

I was intrigued by the looks and description of the so called chicken fish, the only fish in the restaurant that was served fried. It was pretty expensive and was supposed to be good. I quite liked it, but not loved, the method of preparation reminded me too much of what we are used to eat in Polish cuisine.

Grouper is a fish I know well as I’ve seen it a lot while diving. I’ve never been interested in it, it seemed bland and boring when swiming among colourful fishes of warms sees. Its unattractive looks discouraged me from trying it, but as we were ordering it as a group some other people insisted and I got a chance to taste it. It tasted really good!

Do you know monkfish? When I heard the name I immediately thought that it must have been this ugly fish… And I was right! It is simply disgusting in looks but fortunately, it tastes much better than it looks.
Below you can see in in tomato sauce, but the version served with vegetables was much better.

What else? Sardines!
Grilled sardines are the first thing that comes to my mind when I think of Portuguese cuisine (I even bought a mug with three sardines on it). They are very tasty, but fishbones makes eating them a bit uncomfortable.

Bacalhau! Where is bacalhau? – some people might worry right now, thinking I’ve forgotten about it. The only reason I haven’t mentioned this salty cod made in hundreds different ways is that I have no bacalhau in my photos. I tasted it and it was very good, but I tasted it from someone’s elses plate – it was within the reach of my for but a bit too far to take photos. So, please, believe me when I say it was good.

My travel companions were really patient and never protested when I stuck my lens in front of their dish trying to capture what we were about to eat. Some dishes were so good, that we almost wiped off the plates before I realised I had no photos

Shrimps and prawns didn’t use to be my favourite, but I started to like them some time ago, and now I always try them if I can get them fresh. The other dish below? Oh, I don’t remember what it was really, but it was delicious.

Meat, soups and other….

As you can see I spent my Portuguese holidays enjoying fish and seafood, and I was not alone in my choices that’s why I have only two meat dishes in my photo album. The one on the left was sausage prepared in brandy smoke – delicious.

I guess vegetarian don’t have an easy life in Portugal (have a look at aubergine and stuffed champignons below). Whenever I visit countries like Portugal I always think that I’m not going to become a vegetarian because it would limit my options too much.

Soups are worth mentioning as well. My favourite was fish soup as a starter, but I also tried onion soup and soup with bread, garlic and egg (super tasty!).

I had only one thing planned when I was going to Portugal: try pasteis de nata (on the left photo) to check if they are as good as I remembered them to be. They were! No surprise Pasteleria in Belem has been opened since 1837.

Typical Portuguese breakfast didn’t really appeal to me. I might like a coffee and a piece of cake, but not for breakfast!
When dining in Portugal you will be served starters, which you might refuse, but if you eat them, they will be included in your bill.

You might ask how I managed to taste so many things? Simple, I drank wine and wine seems to always help. Because that’s quite a big culinary experience for three and a half days.

I wish I could have stayed there longer as I didn’t manage to try many things, but then I have a reason to go back!
