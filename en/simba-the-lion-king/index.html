Simba – the Lion King.

in Uncategorized

on 16 August 2015

I had no idea I knew how to say ‘lion’ in Swahili – well, it’s so simple! Everybody who’s seen “The Lion King” knows! “Lion” is “Simba” – as simple as that!

Photographing lions on our safari was a great adventure! First, it wasn’t always easy to spot them, then you had to wait till they ‘pose’ for your photo (sometimes they didn’t) and then snap a photo before they turn around and walk off.
In Serengeti Park and Ngorongoro crater cars are not allowed off-road, so if you spot an animal far away there’s no way to leave the road and go closer – you need to wait, it might come closer itself. Or, it might not.
We looked for lions, and they seemed not to pay any attention to us and sometimes they would come closer almost touching the cars and then it was time to look and admire!

The best of all was a pride of lions in Serengeti which we spotted early in the morning on our third day.

There were some more of them hiding – they stood up and without giving us a glace walked in front of the cars disappearing in the grass.

Slowly, they turned towards the rocks and rested there. We followed in the distance.

We saw another lovely group in Ngorongoro crater. First, a lone lion appeared in the road in front of us, and a moment later we saw some more – they marched towards the water, drank some with they bottoms sticking up and then jumped over and disappeared from the view.

It seems to be easy, isn’t it? Well, not really. Most of the time lions wouldn’t come closer and we had to look for them – sometimes even with the binoculars they were not easy to spot as they almost blended with the surroundings.
One of many typical conversations we had in the jeep:
“Lion,” our guide Anton, would suddenly say.
“Where?” we asked after a moment of staring blankly in the direction he was looking.
“Under the bush.”
“OK. Which bush?”
