Safari in Tanznania – Part 2. (Ngorongoro)

in Uncategorized

on 26 September 2015

 

(If you haven’t read the first part yet – click HERE)

At six o’clock we were freezing at the gates leading down to Ngorongoro crater. That day started really early with breakfast at 5.15 and it was a nightmare to get out of my warm sleeping back, but the thought of another good breakfast and a hot cup of tea got me going.
Yes, it was cold at 3200 m above sea level but we hoped it would be much warmer down in the crater. Well, we were wrong, at least at the beginning.

Ngorongoro Crater is a large volcanic caldera, 610 metres (2,000 feet) deep and its floor covers 260 square kilometres (100 square miles). It’s one of the most popular and famous places to see wildlife and that’s exactly why we went there with extremely high expectations. Have a look at some of the photos to see what I saw there during a six-hours’ game ride and read my impressions at the end.

A big herd of wildebeest and the rising sun. Wildebeests usually live in big groups in eastern and south Africa, and they because of their horns and big mane they were called wild beasts or wildebeests  in Africaans.

The pictures above clearly show why it’s worth getting up at the crack of a dawn! There and then we concluded that the Genesis must have looked like that!

 There were lots of lions, just like in Serengeti. Some kept the distance, others strolled along the road next to the jeeps.

 The rhino  was the last one animal we had to see to ‘collect’ The Big Five. I was so happy when we spotted it in the distance, and even more happy when it decided to walk our way and cross the road!

Not everybody manages to see a rhino close up so when we noticed it was walking towards the road, everybody wanted to be as close to it as possible. We were lucky – it crosses the road just two jeeps in front of us. As off-road is not possible in Ngorongoro the jeeps lined along trying to get a better view – I counted over 30 jeeps in total crowded in one place! Lions were also popular with tourists trying to get close.

Do you know what the picture below shows?
I knew by the smell – it was disgustingly unpleasant! Hippo pool again.

I looked as if hippos spent all they time soaking in small ponds, but no, some would walked out to graze around.

There were also vultures at breakfast.

Ostriches:


Hyenas and baby hyenas (I’ll show you the small ones as they’re cute!)
You can’t really see it but they were just playing with a carcass!
Hyenas are scavengers but also good hunters – they manage to hunt around 70% of their food.

A grey crowned crane: – there were many more of them but they didn’t want to pose together for a photo.
A grey crowned crane is also a symbol of Uganda (you can see it on the flag).


Warthogs need to kneel to eat something because they necks are short:

Elephants  were hiding and we drove a few times in the forest before we saw them. And then, as we were moving towards the gate we saw a really big herd of elephants – over thirthy of them together!

There were zebras of course!
Black stripes on white backgroud or white stripes on black background?

The scientists say zebras are black with white stripes. Stripes are supposedly good for camouflage and zebras gather in groups because then they are more difficult to be singled out by the predators.
You can also often see them standing back to front like in the photo above – I read it’s because this way they can spot the danger faster.
So, have a look at a few more zebras:

We saw what’s in the photos and even more. Landscapes were breathtaking and varied,especially if you remember the crater’s diameter is only around 20 km. There are so many animals in this small area that sometimes you might think that you are a visitor in a huge zoo where animals are free to wander, and tourists are stuck in their jeeps like in cages.

I heard some people are disappointed with crowds or with many jeeps chasing animals – if there’s a lion or a rhino close to the road everybody wants to be there and you can only hear shutter sounds. Then I also heard people saying that Ngorongoro was one of their best travel experiences.
I somehow fall in between.
If you ask me about one park to see in your safari trip, I’d say go to Ngorongoro – there’s everything there and wildlife is easy to spot (well, there are no giraffes). However, if you have more money and time go to a less crowded place.
Summing up, I absolutely enjoyed our Ngorongoro game ride, but… Serengeti with its endless plains, serene landscapes and few tourists was so much better!
